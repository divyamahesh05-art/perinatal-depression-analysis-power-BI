# perinatal-depression-analysis-power-BI
# overview
​This study analyzed data from 600 pregnant patients to identify the primary drivers of perinatal depression using the EPDS framework. My findings indicate that 73.1% of the sampled population experienced significant depressive symptoms, highlighting a critical health concern.
. Socio-Economic Impact: There is a significant correlation between financial status and mental health, with women Below the Poverty Line (BPL) showing higher rates of depression.
.​ Family Structure: Interestingly, women living in Joint Family systems reported higher levels of stress compared to those in nuclear families, likely due to increased household responsibilities and reduced privacy.
​. Gestational Peak: A critical finding is that depression symptoms peak around the 28th week of pregnancy, identifying this as the most vulnerable period for clinical intervention.
​. Medical Hisory: While miscarriage is a known stressor, the data shows that a high percentage of depressed patients had no prior miscarriage, suggesting that environmental and economic factors play a more dominant role in this specific population.
#  Methdology
. 1.health-depression   Gravida Status: Used a Horizontal Clustered Bar Chart to identify that multi-pregnant women exhibit a significantly higher prevalence of depression compared to primigravida (first-time) mothers.
.​Maternal Age: Implemented a Stacked Column Chart, revealing that depressive symptoms are most frequent in the 25–29 age group.
​Gestational Age: Utilized an Area Chart to track trends over time, discovering that preterm delivery stages correlate with an increased risk of depression.
. 2. Socio-Demographic Factors

​Family System: Applied a Tree Map to analyze household structures; findings suggest that women in joint family systems have a higher probability of experiencing depression.
​Poverty Line Status: Employed a Pie Chart to visualize economic distribution, showing that those living below the poverty line are more vulnerable.
​Miscarriage History: Used a Donut Chart to evaluate the impact of prior pregnancy loss; the data suggests no dominant correlation between prior miscarriage and current depression levels in this sample.

. ​3. EPDS Clinical Scoring
​Severity Levels: Developed a Stacked Bar Chart to categorize clinical results, indicating that the majority of patients fall within the Mild to Moderate depression range.
# Conclusion
​This analysis of 600 patients confirms that perinatal depression is heavily influenced by socioeconomic and household factors. The data highlights that women in joint family systems and those facing financial instability (below the poverty line) are at the highest risk. Furthermore, the identification of a symptoms peak during the 28th week provides a clear timeline for healthcare providers to offer targeted psychological support.

. ​Uses of this Dashboard
. ​Early Risk Identification: Helps doctors identify high-risk mothers based on their socioeconomic profile before symptoms become severe.
​. Resource Allocation: Assists hospitals in planning mental health screenings specifically around the 28th week of pregnancy.
​Policy Advocacy: Provides data-driven evidence to support the need for better mental health resources for families in lower economic brackets.
